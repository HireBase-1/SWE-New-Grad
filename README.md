## 🔦 Weekly Spotlight

### "Early‑Career Roles that Aim You at Real Systems"

Looking at this week’s 0–2 YOE slice, there’s a clear through‑line: the best openings don’t just say “junior”; they point you straight at **real systems**.

On the product side, **Sigma Computing** and **Asana in Reykjavík** are both explicit about letting new grads work on surfaces that other people depend on – analytics engines and collaboration flows, not just internal admin panels. The stack is familiar (Java/Python/JS at Sigma, modern web + services at Asana), but the impact radius is large.

On the “stack‑y” side, roles like **FreeForm’s C++ new‑grad position in LA** and **Grab’s backend seat on the transaction platform** are refreshingly honest about the guts: C/C++, Linux, RTOS, Go, Redis, DynamoDB, observability tooling. You’re not being shielded from the infrastructure; you’re being invited into it.

And then there are the hybrid roles like **Zettabyte’s full‑stack new‑grad opening**, where you’re expected to jump between Vue/React front‑ends, Go backends, and Kubernetes/GCP wiring. For some people that’s overwhelming; for others it’s exactly the kind of environment that accelerates learning.

If you’re comparing offers right now, one good question to ask is: *“Will I be allowed near the parts of the system that actually matter?”* The best roles in this week’s slice all read like the answer is yes.

## 🧱 What Is Hirebase?

Most job boards drop you into an endless scroll of half‑finished descriptions and
mystery titles. You spend more time reading than actually preparing, and it’s weirdly
hard to answer a basic question like: “who is seriously hiring people like me, right
now?”  Hirebase tries to push that work closer to the machine. It tracks the broader job
market, scrapes and understands millions of postings, and then lets you slice by the
things that actually matter: level, stack, location, compensation ranges, company type.

For new grads, that means you can spend less energy hunting for “maybe this is junior?”
roles and more time on the pieces you control: projects, practice, and thoughtful
applications.  This repo is just one narrow lens – **New Grad SWE** – so you always have
a clean place to start. The rest of the graph lives over on Hirebase.
